# Material Library

This knowledge base is used to store and analyze high-quality videos, articles, images, GIFs, screenshots, and other content references.

The goal is not to copy content. The goal is to understand why a piece of content works, extract reusable patterns, and convert them into original content ideas.

## Supported Material Types

- Short video
- Long video
- Article
- Social post
- Screenshot
- Infographic
- GIF
- Animation
- Landing page
- Report excerpt

## Material Intake Rules

For each material, save or record:

- File name or URL
- Source platform
- Creator or publisher
- Publish time if available
- Topic
- Target audience
- Why it is considered high quality
- Whether it can be publicly referenced
- Whether it is only for private inspiration

## Analysis Dimensions

### 1. Hook

Analyze:

- Opening sentence or first-screen message
- Pain point
- Curiosity gap
- Emotional trigger
- Business relevance

### 2. Structure

Analyze:

- Narrative flow
- Argument structure
- Sequence of points
- Turning point
- Ending or CTA

### 3. Visual Expression

Analyze:

- Frame composition
- Text overlay
- Chart or diagram style
- Motion rhythm
- Color and contrast
- Whether the visual helps understanding

### 4. Business Insight

Analyze:

- What problem it explains
- What assumption it challenges
- What decision it helps the audience make
- Whether it can connect to ad spend diagnosis, agency transparency, or content asset diagnosis

### 5. Reusable Pattern

Extract:

- Reusable title pattern
- Reusable hook pattern
- Reusable structure
- Reusable analogy
- Reusable visual layout
- Reusable CTA

### 6. Adaptation Ideas

Generate:

- Douyin short video version
- WeChat Video Account version
- WeChat article outline
- Infographic version
- Industry-specific adaptation

## Boundaries

- Do not copy scripts directly.
- Do not reuse copyrighted visual assets without permission.
- Do not imitate a creator's unique identity or personal story.
- Extract methods, structures, and insight patterns only.
- Always add your own judgment and business context.
